Course Summary

An easy elective course for those interested in cybersec or just wanna know how some popular hacks are typically carried out (and what you can do to stop them).

This course was just 5 hacking labs, 2 exams, and 1 very easy group project. Since 99% of your time will be spent on the labs, here are how the labs follow (in order):
  1. Buffer Overflow - Difficulty: Very Easy
  2. Tikiwiki Penetration - Difficulty: Hard
  3. Pentesting w/ Armitage - Difficulty: Medium
  4. Heartbleed Attack - Difficulty: Easy
  5. SQL Injection - Difficulty: Very Easy

Course Structure

Depending on which section you enroll in, you will either meet on Tues/Thurs or Mon/Wed/Fri every week. As of Fall 2022, most courses are being held in person - if you are looking to attend online make sure to find a section that has either the "Hybrid" or "Web Online" modes of instruction shown on the student center.

The Group Project

  • Each group must only consist of 2 students each.
  • The project is just a simple slideshow presentation.
  • You must describe in detail 3 major cyber security incidents (hacks, breaches, leaks, etc.).
  • All groups will present on the day before the final exam.
  • This shouldn't take more than maybe an hour total to complete, very easy project.

Opinions on Professor

Views expressed in this section are solely my opinion and it is my goal to provide real, honest feedback with zero BS. Please take my words as one student's overall experience and perspective out of tens of thousands of others.

My Professor: Jun Dai

Professor Dai is not a terrible professor nor is he an amazing professor. But he is very lenient on grading and I've taken 3 of his courses and found them to be very easy compared to the nonsense that you may encounter with other professors. He really hates when students don't understand most topics. Even if it's been 12 weeks since he last covered something, Dai will lose his mind if you ask a question about something he's taught already. Though other than this, he's not very present in the classroom and generally does not care if you screw up a lab/assignment, he will still give you a passing grade on almost everything if you at least tried it. I remember not being able to reach a certain point in the 'Tikiwiki Penetration' hack and he still gave me a 90% on my lab. As mentioned at the top of this page, you will spend 99% of your time on his labs. The material almost doesn't matter since you can find most answers to his exams already written out in his notes. Labs are 75% grammar and spelling, 25% screenshots of successful hacking. If you have decent writing skills, you won't need to do much to pass this course.

TL;DR if you need an easy CSC140+ elective, TAKE THIS COURSE!

Overall Rating

Mostly Positive

An honestly very cool course that teaches both ethical hacking and counter-measures to the most popular hacks that take place every day across the globe. If you are interested in cyber security or just need a really easy elective course to get the stupid elective requirements out of the damn way, this one should be near the top of your priority list. It should be said that the labs were heavily outdated, as they show exploits found over a decade ago and have been public knowledge for many many years. So while you will learn some basic hacking skills, don't expect anything crazy.
