Course Summary

Another essential course that teaches students who have used Windows for the entire lives that Linux exists and can be useful for some development processes and coding in certain languages. This course will mainly focus on programming in C for everything.

There were 2 exams and 8 assignments
  • Assignments
    • The first assignment is just a sanity check to ensure you set up your linux environment properly. The rest of them will be simple programming tasks in C, where the last assignment will test your knowledge on everything.
  • Exams
    • The usual midterm + final exam combo. The midterm was very easy. The final borrowed a lot of questions from the midterm which made it very similar in difficulty. The questions are all meant to be basic conceptual questions and there are a few questions asking you what a block of code does when executed.

Opinions on Professor

Views expressed in this section are solely my opinion and it is my goal to provide real, honest feedback with zero BS. Please take my words as one student's overall experience and perspective out of tens of thousands of others.

My Professor:

Since this was a summer course, he seemed to be in a rush to get us to learn the material but at the same time was very lenient as to whether we understood the material or not. Most concepts were boiled down to "this is how you do it in C, and I will ask you to repeat this exact same thing on the exam so there are no surprises." You can expect the exams to be fairly easy as most students pass this course the first time around with very little resistance. It's not meant to be a difficult one since it's the last lock in the gateway to the Computer Science major. If you're just trying to pass this course without learning a lot, take this professor and move on to your upper division courses.
