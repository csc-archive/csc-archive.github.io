Course Summary

One of the most important courses for acing technical interviews. Some material in CSC28 about recursion is re-introduced in this course. The list of topics covered is as follows:

  • Big-O notation (time & space requirements)
  • Linear lists & multi-lists
  • Stacks, queues, priority queues
  • Binary trees, tree traversals, threaded trees, heaps, binary search trees, AVL trees, B-trees
  • Bit maps, hash maps/tables, union-find
  • Graph traversals, spanning trees, shortest paths
  • Recursion
  • Heap sort, tree sort, radix sort
  • NP-completeness & the P-NP problem

** The topics in bold were far more heavily covered and saw more frequent appearances on exams. Make sure to focus on these.

Opinions on Professor

Views expressed in this section are solely my opinion and it is my goal to provide real, honest feedback with zero BS. Please take my words as one student's overall experience and perspective out of tens of thousands of others.

My Professor: Devin Cook

Professor Cook is considered to be a solid choice for most courses, but I had some serious issues with the lack of timeliness on grading and severe lack of feedback on assignments. After over 14 weeks of waiting, he began grading our assignments at 1:00 AM on a Saturday and threw every student into a massive panick because the average scores were abysmal. Given there was only 1 week left of instruction, there was absolutely no way to fix our assignments in time for the end of the semester since he waited until the last minute to grade them. His style of teaching is honestly very basic with him reading off of slideshows and ocassionally demonstrating how certain algorithms work. He does make various attempts at keeping the class interested in the subject by providing funny quips or videos on topics that would typically be considered highly boring. Though with these negatives out of the way, I personally think Cook is a great dude at heart who has helped fill a small gap in the CSC department's HR problems. With very few quality CSC professors to go around at Sac State, Professor Cook allows the average student just enough leg room to get by without having to dedicate 40 hours per week on his class.

My Overall Rating

Mostly Positive

A pretty useful course that teaches necessary algorithms and analysis skills but is cut slightly short by the lack of practice given to students. The first two assignments are far too large in scope and many students were overbooked with just how demanding they were. Spreading out the assignments in smaller, more detailed modules would have led to a much more stress-free approach on the student's end. It is certainly helpful to understand how each data structure works in theory, but without proper practice it's impossible to ever truly master the material taught in this course which is crucial for technical interviews. Try using HackerRank or Leetcode if you want to extend your knowledge of this course to a more appropriate level.
