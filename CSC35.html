Course Summary

A reasonable course for computer science but one that comes a bit too early. For new coders, this course will be exceptionally difficult unless your understanding of programming is at least of a novice level.

There were 2 exams and 6 assignments

Overall Rating

Mixed

It is difficult to write an objective review for this course because I think the material is important for computer science students to understand but my professor destroyed everyone's interest in it.

Anyway, I think this course teaches some important concepts that are essential for any student pursuing a computing degree of any kind. Though primitive, it gives programmers a few flavors of low level programming and sets the stage for those who want to engage in careers that involve compilers, hardware engineering, and electronics. Some improvements that I think should be made include:

  1. Teaching x64 rather than x86. Intel's x86 architecture is ridiculously complex for undergraduate pre-major students to even attempt to comprehend.
  2. Hiring more professors that have an understanding of modern architectures.
  3. Not requiring a textbook. The internet is chock-full of resources to teach this material for completely free and at a much higher quality.
  4. Not using Visual Studio to code in assembly. Please for the love of God let students use Linux instead of Windows for this class.
